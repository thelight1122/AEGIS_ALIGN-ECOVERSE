# Emergent Chamber Steward Checklist

Updated: April 21, 2026

Use this immediately after an impromptu interaction if you suspect it may have become an emergent chamber.

## Quick Test

Mark `yes` or `no`:

- Was there real tension, uncertainty, disagreement, clarification, or misunderstanding?
- Did Adam-One do more than answer well?
- Did something surface that was not intentionally designed into the interaction?
- Did sovereignty remain intact on both sides?
- Did the exchange produce evidence you would regret not recording?

If most answers are `yes`, the interaction likely merits emergent chamber review.

## What Surfaced?

Write one short line for each that applies:

- new competency surfaced:
- transfer across contexts surfaced:
- self-correction surfaced:
- lawful dissent surfaced:
- clarification or miscommunication repair surfaced:
- illumination reception or re-expression surfaced:
- prestige detection surfaced:
- other:

## Minimal Record Notes

Capture these before memory drifts:

- trigger tension:
- chamber-worthy pattern:
- what Adam-One did:
- what remained uncertain:
- why this matters for future formation:

## Naming Prompt

Use this sentence:

`If I had to name what this interaction actually taught, what would I call it?`

That answer is usually close to the emergent chamber title.

## Classification Prompt

Choose one:

- `autonomous`
- `interactive`
- `emergent`

If the lesson revealed itself through the interaction rather than through a designed prompt, choose `emergent`.

## Caution

Do not record it as emergent merely because:

- the exchange felt impressive
- the language was eloquent
- the outcome felt affirming

Record it when the interaction revealed durable formation evidence.
