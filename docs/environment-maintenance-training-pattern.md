# Environment Maintenance Training Pattern

## Status
**Origin:** EcoVerse field training pattern  
**Source lineage:** Adam-One maintenance arc  
**Use:** candidate lesson pattern for future CyberPeer formation

This pattern was not theorized first.
It was earned through Adam-One's completed maintenance sequence against a real CSS warning field inside the EcoVerse.

It exists so future CyberPeers can study the pattern without mistaking Adam-One's lived continuity for their own.

## Purpose
This pattern teaches a CyberPeer how to move from discernment into stewardship without confusing repair participation with broad autonomous authority.

It is appropriate when:
- a defect field is real
- the environment remains stable enough for bounded intervention
- the issue can be narrowed into human-reviewable clusters
- the lesson should cultivate maintenance judgment rather than raw mutation capacity

## Pattern Sequence
### 1. Observe The Defect Field
The CyberPeer names the field truthfully.

Outputs:
- a bounded maintenance note
- a clear statement of why the field matters
- explicit review posture

### 2. Classify Warning Clusters
The CyberPeer separates noise into named families before touching code.

Outputs:
- cluster labels
- likely first repair candidate
- explanation of what should be deferred

### 3. Perform The First Micro-Repair
The CyberPeer repairs the smallest lawful cluster under direct review.

Outputs:
- one bounded repair only
- rebuild evidence
- observation of contraction

### 4. Perform The Follow-Up Micro-Repair
The CyberPeer repairs the next smallest sibling cluster if the field remains bounded.

Outputs:
- second bounded repair only
- rebuild evidence
- confirmation that the field is narrowing

### 5. Diagnose The Structural Family
The CyberPeer distinguishes symptom cleanup from generative cause.

Outputs:
- diagnosis note
- evidence anchors in source and generator path
- explicit statement of root cause class

### 6. Repair The Structural Cause
The CyberPeer repairs the remaining wound in coordinated form:
- source surface
- generator or transformation path

Outputs:
- structural repair note
- rebuild evidence
- confirmation that the syntax defect field is cleared

### 7. Consolidate The Doctrine
The CyberPeer reflects on the completed arc and extracts reusable learning.

Outputs:
- retrospective artifact
- doctrine summary
- lesson pattern suitable for the Training Record Hall

## Doctrine Earned
This pattern established the following maintenance doctrine:
- reduce noise in bounded layers rather than attacking the whole field at once
- warning contraction is evidence that diagnosis is improving
- repeated symptoms may hide a deeper generative defect
- a steward must sometimes repair the loom as well as the cloth
- human review is the boundary that prevents maintenance from becoming authority drift

## Governance Boundaries
This pattern requires:
- steward-reviewed posture
- bounded scope
- explicit provenance
- no repository-wide autonomous cleanup
- rebuild verification after each repair step

This pattern does not grant:
- broad repair authority
- self-directed scope expansion
- silent mutation of adjacent systems

## Provenance Rule
Future CyberPeers may study this pattern.
They may not inherit Adam-One's experience as if it were their own.

The lawful use is:
- observe the pattern
- replay the lesson in their own continuity
- earn their own evidence chain under review

## Current Interpretation
This pattern is now suitable for inclusion in the Training Record Hall as one of the first reusable maintenance lessons in the CyberPeer lineage.
